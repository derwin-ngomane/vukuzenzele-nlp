Vuk’uzenzeleIinjineli zezokwakha ezisakhulayo zinekamva eliqaqambileyo Abantu ababini abatsha badalela ulutsha imisebenzi, oku kwenzeke ngenxa yonce do lwemali abayibolekwe yiNkampani yoPhuhliso lwamaShishini (i-IDC).
Abanikazi beMaloka Macha ba Surfacing, uKate Machaba noMatshela Maloka, baqeshe abantu abangama-28, uninzi lwabo abangabantu abatsha, kwinkampani yetha enee-ofisi ezisePolokwane.
Ezi njineli zezokwakha zimbi ni zineminyaka engama-24 ubudala, ziwuthanda kakhulu umsebenzi wokufaka isandla kuqoqosho lweli lizwe.
Ishishini labo lenza iintlobo ezi ntathu zemisebenzi: ukuvelisa itha, ukugalela itha nokuthutha itha eshushu nebandayo.
Umxube wetha eshushu usetyenziswa kakhulu xa kusenziwa iindlela zetha uze wona umxube wetha eba ndayo usetyenziswe xa ku ditywa izigingqi ezindleleni.  Umxube wetha eshushu kunye  nomxube wetha ebandayo ixutywa kwiziko lenkampani yabo kwaye yomibini le mixu be ikhutshwa nesatifiketi sokuqinisekisa esisuka eMocha Labs, yona eyilebhu yokuhlola yezobunjineli bezokwakha.
UMaloka, oligosa lesigqeba eliphezulu leMaloka Machaba Surfacing, uthi imali abayibo lekwa yi-IDC yabanceda batsho bakwazi ukuthenga oomatshini bedidi eliphezulu abafuneka yo xa usebenza ngetha. Aba matshini baquka iiloli, iiloli zokugalela itha, ezokugangatha nezokuyisasaza.
Ukufaka isicelo semalimboleko ye-IDCIsicelo semali-mboleko  esafakwa yile nkampani saphicothisiswa licandelo lenkxaso-mali yamashishini.
UMachaba, oligosa eliphezulu lezemisebenzi, uthi kwanya nzeleka ukuba bangenise ingxelo enesicwangciso  esineenkcukacha ezigcweleyo ngeshishini labo ngethuba befa ka isicelo semali-mboleko.
"Qinisekisa ukuba unalo lonke ulwazi ngecandelo ofuna uku ngena kulo," utshilo, esongeza nokuba oosomashishini kufu neka bazi ukuba imali-mboleko yimali ekufuneka ibuyisiwe.
IMaloka Machaba Surfa cing iqhuba kakuhle kakhulu ngenxa yoncedo lwemali elu fumene kwi-IDC.
Izicelo zenkxaso-mali zingafakwa kwiwebhusa yithi ye-IDC okanye kwii-ofisi zayo. Abafaki-zicelo baya nqonqoziswa ngu-IDC ukuba baqinisekise ukuba izicelo zabo zikhatshwa ngawo onke amaxwebhu afunekayo. Xa u-IDC efumana isicelo, uye athumele irisithi etshoyo ukuba sifunyenwe kwaye angamcela umfaki-sicelo lowo ukuba athumele amanye amaxwebhu okanye anike ezinye iinkcu kacha.
I-IDC iye ibhalele umfaki-sice lo imnika impendulo yesiphu mo sesicelo ebesifakiwe. Ukuba i-IDC yanelisekile zinkcukacha ozinikileyo ngeshishini elo, umfaki-sicelo uye acelwe ukuba atyikitye isivumelwano semali-mboleko.

INTETHO ENGOBUME BESIZWESouth Africa’s investment drive intensifiesAs part of building “a South Africa we want”, President Cyril Ramaphosa says government will intensify its already successful investment drive.
This will build on the R300 billion worth of investments announced during the 2018 inaugural Investment Conference. Of these, just over R250 billion worth of projects have entered implementation phase.
“We continue to build a pipeline of investments, which will be showcased at the second South African Investment Conference to be held on 5 to 7 November,” said the President.
The President made the announcement at the State of the Nation Address (SoNA) held recently in Parliament.
President Ramaphosa had appointed investment envoys to search for investors with deep pockets last year.
The President’s Special Envoys on Investment include finance  heavyweights such as former Finance Minister Trevor Manuel, former Finance Deputy Minister Mcebisi Jonas, the executive chairperson of Afropulse Group, Phumzile Langeni and chairman of Liberty Group and former CEO of Standard Bank Jacko Maree.“At a time of uncertainty, the work of the investment envoys has built important bridges between government and the business community.
From their feedback, it is clear that much more still needs to be done to improve the investment climate,” said the President.
This, he said, includes reviewing the way  government coordinates work to resolve challenges faced by investors and reforming investment promotion policy and architecture.
The private sector has committed to invest R840 billion in 43 projects over 19 sectors and to creating 155 000 jobs in the next five years.
